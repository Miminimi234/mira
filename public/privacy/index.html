Privacy Policy

Last updated: 2025-11-28

This Privacy Policy describes how Mira ("we", "our", "us") collects, uses, discloses, and protects personal information when you use our website, services, and applications (collectively, the "Service"). By using the Service you agree to the collection and use of information in accordance with this policy.

1. Scope

This policy applies to personal information collected through our website, mobile apps, APIs, and other interaction channels. It does not apply to information collected offline or by third parties that we do not control.

2. Information We Collect

We collect information you provide directly and information collected automatically when you use the Service.

2.1 Information You Provide

  • Account information: name, email address, username, password, profile data.
  • Payment information: billing address and payment token (we do not store full card numbers) when you purchase paid features. Payments are processed by third-party processors (see below).
  • Communications: support requests, feedback, and other messages you send us.
  • Content you submit: any content you upload or post through the Service (including images, text, and files).

2.2 Information Collected Automatically

  • Usage data: pages visited, features used, timestamps, session durations, and interactions.
  • Device & technical data: IP address, browser or app type and version, operating system, device identifiers, and system settings.
  • Cookies and similar technologies: to provide and personalize the Service and for analytics.

3. How We Use Information

We use personal information for the following purposes:

  • To provide, operate, and maintain the Service.
  • To create and manage your account, authenticate access, and provide customer support.
  • To process payments and prevent fraud.
  • To monitor, analyze, and improve our features and Service performance.
  • To send transactional messages, updates, and marketing communications (where you have consented).
  • To comply with legal obligations and enforce our terms and policies.

4. Lawful Basis for Processing (EEA/UK)

If you are in the European Economic Area (EEA) or the UK, we rely on one or more of the following legal bases to process your personal data: your consent; performance of a contract; compliance with a legal obligation; our legitimate interests (for example, to operate and improve the Service, prevent fraud, and secure our systems).

5. Sharing and Disclosure

We do not sell personal data. We may share personal information with:

  • Service providers: hosting providers, payment processors, analytics providers, email and messaging vendors, and other vendors who perform services on our behalf.
  • Business transfers: in connection with a merger, acquisition, financing, or sale of assets.
  • Legal reasons: to comply with laws, respond to lawful requests, or protect rights, property, or safety.
  • With your consent: when you ask us to share information or we otherwise disclose it with your permission.

6. Third-Party Services and Integrations

We use third-party services to operate and improve the Service. These vendors may receive Personal Information as processors. Common categories include:

  • Hosting & Infrastructure: (e.g., AWS, DigitalOcean, Vercel)
  • Identity & Authentication: (e.g., Google, GitHub, Apple sign-in)
  • Payments: (e.g., Stripe, PayPal)
  • Analytics & Crash Reporting: (e.g., Google Analytics, Sentry, Plausible)
  • Email & Messaging: (e.g., SendGrid, Mailgun, Twilio)

These third parties have their own privacy policies. We recommend reviewing them for details on how they handle your data.

7. Cookies and Tracking

We and our partners use cookies and similar technologies to provide, personalize, and protect the Service, and for analytics. You can manage cookie preferences through your browser settings or device settings. For details on specific cookies used, please contact us.

8. Data Retention

We retain personal information as long as necessary to provide the Service, for legitimate business purposes, to comply with legal obligations, and to resolve disputes. When data is no longer required, we delete or anonymize it.

9. International Transfers

We may transfer personal information to countries outside your jurisdiction for the purposes described in this policy. When transferring personal data from the EEA/UK, we implement safeguards such as standard contractual clauses or rely on adequacy decisions where available.

10. Security

We implement reasonable administrative, technical, and physical safeguards to protect personal information. No method of transmission or storage is 100% secure; we cannot guarantee absolute security. We maintain incident response practices and will notify affected users and authorities as required by law in the event of a data breach.

11. Your Rights

Depending on your location, you may have rights regarding your personal information, including:

  • Access: request a copy of the personal data we hold about you.
  • Correction: request correction of inaccurate or incomplete data.
  • Deletion: request deletion of your personal data (subject to legal exceptions).
  • Portability: request a machine-readable copy of your data.
  • Restriction & Objection: object to or request restriction of processing in certain circumstances.
  • Withdraw consent: where processing is based on consent, you may withdraw it (this does not affect processing already completed).

To exercise these rights, contact us at privacy@miramaps.io. We may need to verify your identity before fulfilling requests. We aim to respond within applicable statutory timeframes.

12. Children

Our Service is not directed to children under the age of 13 (or higher age where required). We do not knowingly collect information from children. If you believe we have collected information from a child, please contact us to request deletion.

13. California Privacy Rights

If you are a California resident, you may have additional rights under the CCPA/CPRA, including the right to request information about the categories of personal information we have collected or sold, and the right to opt-out of the sale of your personal information. We do not sell personal information for monetary consideration. To exercise California privacy rights, contact privacy@miramaps.io and include "California Privacy Rights" in the subject line.

14. International Users

By using the Service, you consent to the transfer of your information to the United States and other countries where our service providers operate. Those countries may have data protection laws different from your own.

15. Automated Decision-Making

We may use automated systems to process data for operational purposes (e.g., fraud detection, personalization). If you have questions about automated decisions that significantly affect you, contact us.

16. Links to Other Sites

The Service may contain links to third-party websites and services. This Privacy Policy does not apply to those third parties. We encourage you to read their privacy policies before providing personal information.

17. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. If we make material changes, we will provide notice via the Service or email where appropriate. The "Last updated" date at the top of this page indicates when the policy was last revised.
